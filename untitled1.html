untitled

july 12, 2020 ★ personal essay

I walk and breathe, sleep and eat.
I wake up, I work, I dream.
I exist.
But do I live?
No.
Hell, this sounds like the set up to a riddle, come to think of it.
Maybe it is.
After all, we are riddles, paradoxes, contradictions.
What comes of work but more life?
What comes of life but more work?
Why do we stay around in the first place,
When we merely survive with the goal of staying alive?
And so I walk, and breathe, and sleep, and eat.
And wake up, and work, and dream.
And I exist.
But I do not live.
I have never lived.
Nobody has ever lived within our society.
The scientific definition of life implies some sort of autonomy,
Of which I have none.
We want so desperately to have control.
To have power.
To choose.
People have fought and died in wars for this cause,
Sacrificed so much and suffered so horribly,
All for a tiny glimpse, a morsel of a peek at it.
At freedom.
Freedom in any way, shape, or form.
People will tell you that we have won these battles.
That we are already free.
That we make choices, and therefore are free.
I choose between starving and drowning, am I free?
Hey, that seems like a pretty simple riddle.
We work our whole lives so that we can hope to savour our last years on Earth undisturbed.
To savour the glowing light of control,
While the light of existence fades from us.
Maybe it’s not a light at the end of the tunnel.
Maybe it’s the window to the factory where the cogs are made.
Those who are on the edge of the ultimate liberation peer inside,
They see the young metal molded into shape.
They see it cooled, hardened, turned into every other cog.
All placed in a large machine.
Turning, turning, turning.
To run a gargantuan machine, for they are afraid of what happens when it stops.
It’s all they know, it’s all they do.
But what else is there to know or to do?
When you were molded all your life to serve the purpose of the cog?
And so how are we to know a world beyond the walls?
Beyond the piles of money?
Beyond the mountains of capital?
We are not free relative to them.
Not at all, nope, not in the slightest.
It’s actually quite the opposite, in fact:
We turn and turn and run and run for them,
If we grind to a halt then we are cast out of the machine and are labeled “damaged”
We turn in time with one another.
A collective ballet of slaves,
Arms interlocked,
Fingers woven together
All dancing to the somber but all-too-familiar rhythm of:
“You have to work hard in order for success to come about!”
What is success?
Oh, I see.
It’s bought.
Everything is bought.
Everything is sold.
Ah, what a freedom it is to consume and to be consumed!
My fellow people do not know me.
They know a spectacle of me.
They know the me that I produce for their consumption.
To generate a social capital, of sorts.
This me becomes me
Here’s another paradox, then!
I am a slave to myself!
But how can that be?
Honestly, who even cares at this point?
I function not as a human, but as a relation.
So what’s even the point of finding my true self?
Financial relations are kind of similar to social relations in this sense, right?
I make money.
I am valued by my skill at making money.
I hope that makes sense.
Actually, you know what, no I don’t.
Nothing makes sense so why should I?
After all, the whole point of a paradox is that it doesn’t make any sense at all!
And so I walk.
And I breathe.
And I sleep.
And I eat.
And I wake up.
And I work.
And I dream.
And, of course, I exist.
But do I live?
Do I live in the sense that I have my own choices?
That I can be my real self?
That I can exist not through other people or other objects or other commodities,
But alongside others?
No, I do not live.
Aw shit, I’m rambling again, aren’t I?
Fuck, I really gotta lighten up.


logan king ◦ he/him
