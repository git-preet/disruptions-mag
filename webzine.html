on the refusal to acknowledge white privilege

"The murders of George Floyd, Breonna Taylor, Elijah McClain, and hundreds of others at the hands of the police have incited many people to confront their privilege and spread awareness on their Instagram stories and Twitter accounts. I can't help but wonder: why did it take the murder of innocent black men, black womxn, black children, and other people of color for white people to realize that they have privilege?"

"After all, we are riddles, paradoxes, contradictions.
What comes of work but more life?
What comes of life but more work?
Why do we stay around in the first place,
When we merely survive with the goal of staying alive?
And so I walk, and breathe, and sleep, and eat.
And wake up, and work, and dream.
And I exist.
But I do not live.
I have never lived.
Nobody has ever lived within our society."

logan king ◦ july 12, 2020
