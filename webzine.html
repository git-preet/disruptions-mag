Tyler, the Creator

ALBUM: Cherry Bomb

Released April 3, 2015

"Cherry Bomb,"" Tyler, the Creator's fourth studio album, opens up with a catchy tune called "DEATHCAMP,"" featuring Cole Alexander. With a repetitive yet enticing guitar riff, Tyler starts this album much stronger than he did "Wolf."" The next three songs, "BUFFALO," "PILOT,"" and "RUN,"" have a classic, ominous undertone. Though the rapping may not be too harsh, the strong synth backgrounds leave you on the edge of your seat, YOUR WINGS,"" featuring Roy Ayers, Syd, and Kali Uchis. This is personally my favorite on the album. There's a long introduction with just mallet percussion and electric guitar, which leads into Kali Uchis' smooth, sultry vocals. Her lilting voice combined with Tyler's harsher rapping is a lovely combination. The album continues through a few more songs, which show Tyler's more experimental side, using different synths and odd instruments. The album finishes off with "OKAGA, CA. Like in Wolf, this album ends on a softer note, with simple backgrounds and interesting lyrics. Cherry Bomb, in my opinion, is better than Wolf, simply because of the presence of more musical songs.
